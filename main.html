Unveiling Genetically Influenced Metabotypes for Gastric Cancer Risk Stratification: A Multi-Omics Study with Preliminary Insights into Primary Prevention

Zong-Chao Liu1, Heng-min Xu1, Meng-Yuan Wang1, Wei-Cheng You1, Kai-Feng Pan1, Wen-Qing Li1

State Key Laboratory of Holistic Integrative Management of Gastrointestinal Cancers, Beijing Key Laboratory of Carcinogenesis and Translational Research, Department of Cancer Epidemiology, Peking University Cancer Hospital & Institute, Beijing, China

We have developed an interactive panel for visualizing the complex relationships of the NMR biomarkers for gastric cancer. Click to navigate to the panel.

  • A Gaussian graph model was applied to estimate the partial correlation among NMR blood metabolic biomarkers, as sourced from Nightingale Health.
  • The connections (edges) between these NMR blood metabolic biomarkers were established based on the partial correlations. These correlations were determined using a Gaussian graph model constructed from data of over 270,000 UK Biobank (UKB) participants.
  • Node relationships between expression quantitative trait loci (eQTLs) and the NMR metabolic biomarkers were identified based on the single nucleotide polymorphisms (SNPs) associated with these biomarkers.
  • The sankey diagram on the right illustrates the mapping route from the NMR metabolic biomarkers to the LC-MS metabolic profiles. Only Pearson correlation coefficients with absolute values greater than 0.15 are displayed on the graph.
  • Odds ratios for Gastric cancer (or Early Gastric Cancer) versus mild precancerous gastric lesions were estimated for the mapped LC-MS metabolites. These estimations were adjusted for various factors, including age, sex, H. Pylori infection, and lifestyle factors like smoking and drinking.
